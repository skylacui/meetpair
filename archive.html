Our inaugural PAIR event in 2025 was a huge success. Twelve mentors and twelve mentees came together for a morning that none of us expected to feel so significant. The room had an energy that was hard to name — somewhere between nervousness and possibility — and it settled into something warm almost immediately. The Munch & Learn set the tone early — mentors sharing their unfiltered career stories over breakfast, mentees realizing these accomplished women once felt just as uncertain as they did. There was something quietly powerful about hearing "I didn't know what I was doing either" from someone who clearly figured it out.

By the PAIR Activity, the room had shifted. Small groups huddled together, trading questions about interviews, handshakes, and the parts of 'adulting' no one teaches you. Mentees who had arrived quiet were leaning in, asking follow-ups, taking notes in their phones.

The Ice Cream Social wound things down, but the conversations kept going. Several mentors stayed long after the scheduled end time — exchanging contacts, making promises to follow up, already talking about next year. What started as an event had become something more like a beginning.

Looking back, PAIR '25 was never really about the agenda. It was about twelve girls leaving with a slightly wider sense of what's possible — and twelve women reminded of how far a single afternoon can go. We left that morning knowing we'd be back.
